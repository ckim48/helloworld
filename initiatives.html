// VS exploratory

Q1. What do we do there?

During Monday KSCR time, we teach village school students the basics of coding. After some lessons, they will start making their own games with Scratch.

Q2. What have we done so far?

Last year, we led an exploratory for 2nd and 3rd graders for one season and led an exploratory for 4th and 5th graders for another. We taught the students basics in coding and helped them make their own games through Scratch.

Q3. How can members participate?

After the lessons are over, the members can come to the exploratory sessions to help each student make their own game. The time would be spent answering their questions and helping them with their code.

If you cannot participate during Monday KSCR time, you can help make the slides used for the lessons, or help fix students’ code by accessing their Scratch accounts. You can do this anywhere.

Qualifications for helping with exploratory work include a basic knowledge of coding, and some fluency in Scratch.
